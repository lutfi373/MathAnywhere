Use this guide if you're having trouble with the questions:

Question 1: What does the second derivative measure?

Answer: "The second derivative measures the rate of change of the slope."

Other acceptable words: first derivative, rate of change

Explanation: The first derivative gives us the slope at any point, and the second derivative tells us how that slope is changing.

Question 2: When f''(x) > 0

Correct Answer: A) The graph is concave up (curves upward like a smile)

Explanation: Positive second derivative means the function curves upward, like the shape of a smile ‚ò∫Ô∏è

Question 3: Second derivative of x¬≥

Answer: f''(x) = 6x

Step-by-step:

  • f(x) = x¬≥
  • f'(x) = 3x¬≤ (power rule)
  • f''(x) = 6x (power rule again)
Question 4: Physics - Second derivative of position

Answer: acceleration

Physics connection:

  • Position: s(t)
  • Velocity: s'(t) = ds/dt
  • Acceleration: s''(t) = d¬≤s/dt¬≤
Question 5: f''(3) for f(x) = 2x¬≤ + 4x + 1

Answer: 4

Step-by-step:

  • f'(x) = 4x + 4
  • f''(x) = 4 (constant!)
  • f''(3) = 4 (since f''(x) is constant)
