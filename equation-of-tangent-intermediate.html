Question 1: Tangent to sin(x) at œÄ/4

Answer: $y = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}x + \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(1 - \frac{\pi}{4})$

Step-by-step:

  • $f(\frac{\pi}{4}) = \sin(\frac{\pi}{4}) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
  • $f'(x) = \cos(x)$, so $f'(\frac{\pi}{4}) = \cos(\frac{\pi}{4}) = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$
  • Point-slope form: $y - \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2} = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}(x - \frac{\pi}{4})$
Question 2: Tangent to eÀ£ at x = 0

Answer: $y = x + 1$

Explanation: For $f(x) = e^x$, $f'(x) = e^x$. At $x = 0$: $f(0) = 1$, $f'(0) = 1$

Question 3: Chain rule with (2x + 1)¬≥

Answer: 6

Explanation: $f'(x) = 3(2x + 1)^2 \cdot 2 = 6(2x + 1)^2$. At $x = 0$: $f'(0) = 6(1)^2 = 6$

Question 4: Normal line to x¬≤ at (2,4)

Answer: $y = -\frac{1}{4}x + \frac{9}{2}$

Explanation: Tangent slope = $f'(2) = 4$, so normal slope = $-\frac{1}{4}$

Question 5: Slope of tangent to ln(x) at x = e

Answer: $\frac{1}{e}$

Explanation: $\frac{d}{dx}[\ln(x)] = \frac{1}{x}$, so at $x = e$, slope = $\frac{1}{e}$
