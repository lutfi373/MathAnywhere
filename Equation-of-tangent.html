2.6 Equation of Tangent to a Curve

📐 2.6.1 Tangent Line

Based on the graph below, tangent line is the straight line that touches the curve, y = f(x) at one point, (x₀, f(x₀)).

📋 2.6.2 Determine Equation of a Tangent to a Curve

Step-by-Step Process:

  1. Step 1: Find the derivative, dy/dx or f'(x)
  2. Step 2: Calculate the slope of the tangent by substituting x₀ into dy/dx or f'(x)
  3. Step 3: Determine the equation of the tangent, y = mx + c

Calculate the slope at x = 2

Part B: Find the equation of the tangent line

We know: slope m = 1/2, point (2, 1)

Use y = mx + c, substitute the point to find c

What is the equation of the tangent line?

Answer Format

Linear equation with fractional coefficient
Hint: 1 = (1/2)(2) + c → 1 = 1 + c → c = 0. So y = (1/2)x
