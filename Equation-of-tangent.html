What is a Tangent Line?

A tangent line is a straight line that touches a curve at exactly one point, called the point of tangency. At this point, the tangent line has the same slope as the curve, making it a powerful tool for understanding the curve's behavior at that specific point.

Key Concepts

1. Point of Tangency

The point where the tangent line touches the curve. At this point:

  • The line and curve intersect exactly once
  • The line and curve share the same slope
  • The line represents the best linear approximation of the curve at that point
2. Slope at a Point

The slope of the tangent line equals the derivative of the function at the point of tangency:

\[ \text{Slope} = f'(x_1) \] where \(x_1\) is the x-coordinate of the point of tangency
3. Point-Slope Form

The equation of the tangent line can be written using the point-slope form:

\[ y - y_1 = m(x - x_1) \] where:
  • \((x_1, y_1)\) is the point of tangency
  • \(m = f'(x_1)\) is the slope at that point
4. Slope-Intercept Form

We can rewrite the tangent line equation in slope-intercept form:

\[ y = mx + b \] where:
  • \(m = f'(x_1)\) is the slope
  • \(b = y_1 - mx_1\) is the y-intercept

Worked Example

Let's find the equation of the tangent line to \(f(x) = x^2\) at the point (2, 4).

For \(f(x) = x^2\), the derivative is \(f'(x) = 2x\)

At x = 2: \(f'(2) = 2(2) = 4\), so the slope is 4

Using point-slope form with (2, 4) and slope = 4:

\[ y - 4 = 4(x - 2) \] \[ y = 4x - 4 \]

This is the equation of the tangent line in slope-intercept form.

Important Notes

  • The tangent line represents the instantaneous rate of change at a point
  • A horizontal tangent line (slope = 0) indicates a potential maximum or minimum point
  • A vertical tangent line (undefined slope) indicates a potential vertical asymptote or cusp
  • The tangent line is the best linear approximation of the function near the point of tangency

How do we convert a square root to power form?
Remember: \(\sqrt{x} = x^{\frac{1}{2}}\)
So, \(\sqrt{x-1} = (x-1)^{\frac{1}{2}}\)

Part A, Step 2: Find \(f'(x)\) using the power rule

How do we differentiate a power function?
Use the power rule: \( \frac{d}{dx}[(x-1)^{\frac{1}{2}}] = \frac{1}{2}(x-1)^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x-1}} \)

Part A, Step 3: Calculate the slope at \(x = 2\)

Substitute \(x = 2\) into \(f'(x) = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{x-1}}\)

How do we find the slope at a specific point?
Substitute x = 2 into f'(x):
f'(2) = \( \frac{1}{2\sqrt{2-1}} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{1}} = \frac{1}{2} \)

Part B: Find the equation of the tangent line

We know: slope \(m = \)\(\frac{1}{2}\), point \((2, 1)\)

Use \(y = mx + c\), substitute the point to find \(c\)

How do we find the equation of a tangent line?
Use \(y = mx + c\)
Substitute point \((2,1)\) and slope \(m = \frac{1}{2}\):
\(1 = \frac{1}{2}(2) + c\)
\(1 = 1 + c\)
\(c = 0\)
Therefore, \(y = \frac{1}{2}x\)
