📝 UiTM Calculus I - Implicit Differentiation Answer Guide

These questions are aligned with UiTM Calculus I syllabus covering implicit differentiation techniques:

Question 1: MCQ - Circle Equation Implicit Differentiation

Answer: $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{3-x}{y+2}$

Topic: Basic implicit differentiation of circle equations

Solution: $2x + 2y\frac{dy}{dx} - 6 + 4\frac{dy}{dx} = 0 \Rightarrow (2y + 4)\frac{dy}{dx} = 6 - 2x$

Question 2: Drag & Drop - Product Rule with Implicit Differentiation

Answer: $2xy + x^2\frac{dy}{dx} + y^2 + 2xy\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$

Topic: Combining product rule with implicit differentiation

Solution: Apply product rule to each term: $\frac{d}{dx}(x^2y) = 2xy + x^2\frac{dy}{dx}$

Question 3: MathQuill - Tangent Line Slope

Answer: $-\frac{3}{4}$ or $-0.75

Topic: Finding tangent line slopes using implicit differentiation

Solution: $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x}{y}$, at $(3,4)$: $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{3}{4}$

Question 4: MCQ - Related Rates (Oil Spill)

Answer: $\frac{dA}{dt} = 400\pi$ m²/min

Topic: Related rates problems with circular areas

Solution: $\frac{dA}{dt} = 2\pi r \frac{dr}{dt} = 2\pi(100)(2) = 400\pi$

Question 5: Drag & Drop - Ladder Problem (Related Rates)

Answer: $2x\frac{dx}{dt} + 2y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$, when $x=5$, $y=12$, so $\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{5}{6}$ m/s

Topic: Classic ladder sliding problem using related rates

Solution: From $x^2 + y^2 = 169$, differentiate to get $x\frac{dx}{dt} + y\frac{dy}{dt} = 0$
